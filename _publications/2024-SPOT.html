--- layout: publication authors: - Shivani Kumar - Rishabh Gupta - Md Shad Akhtar - Tanmoy Chakraborty highlight: true pdf: https://aclanthology.org/2024.lrec-main.37.pdf link: https://github.com/LCS2-IIITD/SPOT.git tags: - Adding SPICE to Life: Speaker Profiling in Multiparty Conversations - Rishabh Gupta - Md Shad Akhtar - Tanmoy Chakraborty - Joint International Conference on Computational Linguistics, Language Resources and Evaluation - LREC-COLING - Conference - 2024 title: "Adding SPICE to Life: Speaker Profiling in Multiparty Conversations" type: - Conference venue: LREC-COLING venue_location: Torino, Italia venue_tags: - LREC-COLING venue_url: https://lrec-coling-2024.org/ year: 2024 date: 2024-02-01 --- In the realm of conversational dynamics, individual idiosyncrasies challenge the suitability of a one-size-fits-all approach for dialogue agent responses. Prior studies often assumed the speaker’s persona’s immediate availability, a premise not universally applicable. To address this gap, we explore the Speaker Profiling in Conversations (SPC) task, aiming to synthesize persona attributes for each dialogue participant. SPC comprises three core subtasks: persona discovery, persona-type identification, and persona-value extraction. The first subtask identifies persona-related utterances, the second classifies specific attributes, and the third extracts precise values for the persona. To confront this multifaceted challenge, we’ve diligently compiled SPICE, an annotated dataset, underpinning our thorough evaluation of diverse baseline models. Additionally, we benchmark these findings against our innovative neural model, SPOT, presenting an exhaustive analysis encompassing a nuanced assessment of quantitative and qualitative merits and limitations.
